The Authors

Six directors
worth following.

Thailand's directors are unusual in operating at every register simultaneously — slow-cinema auteurs and box-office magicians often share crews, financiers, and ambitions.

Apichatpong Weerasethakulอภิชาติพงศ์ วีระเศรษฐกุล

Palme d'Or winner. Operates somewhere between dream, jungle, and memory. Globally, Thailand's most celebrated arthouse export.

Uncle Boonmee · Memoria · Tropical Malady
Nattawut Poonpiriyaนัฐวุฒิ พูนพิริยะ

Genre architect. Made Bad Genius a global heist film and One for the Road a meditation on dying friends. GDH's auteur-in-residence.

Bad Genius · One for the Road · Countdown
Banjong Pisanthanakunบรรจง ปิสัญธนะกูล

Crossover commercial director: helmed both record-breaking horror (The Medium) and the highest-grossing Thai film of its decade (Pee Mak).

The Medium · Pee Mak · Shutter
Pat Boonnitipatพัฒน์ บุญนิธิพัฒน์

Debut feature became the all-time highest-grossing Thai film internationally. A new generation's exemplar of mass-emotional precision.

How to Make Millions Before Grandma Dies
Phuttiphong Aroonphengพุทธิพงษ์ อรุณเพ็ง

Won Venice Orizzonti at his debut. Cinematographer-turned-director with a painterly, politically charged eye on the Thai-Myanmar border.

Manta Ray · Morrison
Anocha Suwichakornpongอโนชา สุวิชากรพงศ์

Locarno-honored. Cinema as historical reckoning. Co-founder of Electric Eel Films, a key independent production house.

By the Time It Gets Dark · Krabi, 2562
